## Source Evaluation Criteria

When evaluating a new source, score it on these dimensions:

| Criterion | Weight | Questions to Ask |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Authority** | 30% | Is this a recognized authority in the field? |
| **Recency** | 25% | How current is the data? |
| **Methodology** | 20% | Is the methodology transparent? |
| **Bias** | 15% | Does the source have commercial bias? |
| **Corroboration** | 10% | Can findings be verified elsewhere? |

### Trust Level Definitions

| Level | Definition | Citation Requirement |
|-------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Highest** | Industry standard, peer-reviewed | Single source sufficient for claims |
| **High** | Established reputation, transparent methodology | Prefer 2+ sources for key claims |
| **Medium** | Useful but may have bias or limited scope | Always corroborate with 2nd source |
| **Low** | Unverified, anecdotal, or self-reported | Never use as sole source |
