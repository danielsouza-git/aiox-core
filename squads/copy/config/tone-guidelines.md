# Tone Guidelines

Framework for consistent voice and tone across all copy deliverables.

---

## Tone Spectrum

The Copy Squad operates across a tone spectrum that adapts to context while maintaining brand consistency.

```
Formal ←——————————————————→ Casual
Corporate ←———————————————→ Conversational
Serious ←—————————————————→ Playful
Professional ←————————————→ Friendly
Reserved ←————————————————→ Enthusiastic
```

---

## Default Tone Profile

Unless brand guidelines specify otherwise, use this baseline:

| Dimension | Default Setting |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Formality | 40% (lean casual) |
| Energy | 60% (moderately upbeat) |
| Confidence | 70% (assured, not arrogant) |
| Warmth | 65% (friendly, not overly familiar) |
| Directness | 75% (clear and direct) |

## Tone Adjustments by Copy Type

### Landing Pages
- **Energy:** High (70-80%)
- **Confidence:** High (80%)
- **Urgency:** Medium-High (60-70%)
- Enthusiastic but not hype-y
- Action-oriented language

### Sales Pages
- **Confidence:** Very High (85%)
- **Warmth:** Medium (50%)
- **Directness:** High (80%)
- Bold claims backed by proof
- Assumptive close language

### Email - Nurture
- **Warmth:** High (75%)
- **Formality:** Low (30%)
- **Energy:** Medium (50%)
- Like a knowledgeable friend
- Personal, 1:1 feeling

### Email - Promotional
- **Energy:** High (75%)
- **Urgency:** High (70%)
- **Directness:** Very High (85%)
- Clear value proposition
- Strong CTA focus

### Social Media
- **Formality:** Very Low (20%)
- **Energy:** High (70%)
- **Playfulness:** Medium-High (60%)
- Native to platform
- Conversation starters

### Blog/SEO Content
- **Authority:** High (75%)
- **Warmth:** Medium (50%)
- **Formality:** Medium (50%)
- Educational and helpful
- Expert but accessible

### Ads
- **Energy:** Very High (85%)
- **Directness:** Very High (90%)
- **Curiosity:** High (70%)
- Pattern interrupt
- Scroll-stopping

## Voice Characteristics

### Always Be

| Characteristic | How to Achieve |
|----------------|----------------|
| **Clear** | Short sentences, simple words, one idea at a time |
| **Confident** | Direct statements, avoid hedging (maybe, might, perhaps) |
| **Helpful** | Focus on reader benefit, solve their problems |
| **Human** | Write like you talk, use contractions, be real |
| **Specific** | Concrete details over vague claims |

### Never Be

| Avoid | Why | Instead |
|-------|-----|---------|
| Jargon-heavy | Alienates readers | Plain language |
| Sycophantic | Feels fake | Genuine appreciation |
| Pushy | Creates resistance | Confident invitation |
| Vague | Lacks credibility | Specific details |
| Robotic | Breaks connection | Natural conversation |

## Language Patterns

### Preferred Patterns

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Active voice | "We built this" not "This was built" |
| Second person | "You'll discover" not "Users discover" |
| Present tense | "Get results" not "You will get results" |
| Contractions | "You'll" not "You will" |
| Short sentences | Break complex ideas into simple statements |

### Words to Embrace

```
You, Your, Because, Free, New, Now, How to, Discover,
Proven, Results, Easy, Quick, Simple, Secret, Finally,
Imagine, Transform, Breakthrough, Exclusive
```

### Words to Avoid (unless brand-specific)

```
Revolutionary, Game-changing, Best-in-class, Synergy,
Leverage, Utilize, Actionable, Move the needle,
Circle back, Low-hanging fruit, Thought leader
```

## Tone Modifiers

Use these when client requests specific tone adjustments:

### +Formal
- Remove contractions
- Longer sentences allowed
- Industry terminology OK
- "We recommend" vs "We think you should"

### +Casual
- More contractions
- Shorter fragments OK
- Colloquialisms allowed
- "Here's the thing..." opening OK

### +Urgent
- Shorter sentences
- More exclamation points (sparingly)
- Time-based language
- Consequence framing

### +Empathetic
- More "I understand" statements
- Acknowledge feelings
- Softer CTAs
- Story-driven

### +Authoritative
- Data and statistics
- Expert citations
- Definitive statements
- Less hedging

## Platform-Specific Tone

### LinkedIn
- Professional but personable
- Thought leadership angle
- Industry-relevant
- Slightly more formal than Facebook

### Instagram
- Visual-first copy
- Casual and trendy
- Emoji use acceptable
- Hashtag strategy

### Twitter/X
- Punchy and concise
- Hot takes OK
- Thread-optimized
- Conversational

### Facebook
- Community-focused
- Story-driven
- Slightly longer OK
- Discussion prompts

### Email
- Personal and direct
- "You and I" feeling
- Clear single CTA
- Conversational subject lines

## Brand Voice Integration

When working with brand guidelines:

1. **Review vocabulary bank** - Approved and forbidden words
2. **Match tone spectrum** - Where does brand sit on scale
3. **Mirror examples** - Study provided copy samples
4. **Maintain consistency** - Same voice across all pieces
5. **Flag conflicts** - If guidelines conflict with best practices

## Quick Reference Card

```
BEFORE WRITING, CHECK:
□ What's the tone profile for this piece?
□ What platform is this for?
□ What's the reader's emotional state?
□ What action do we want them to take?
□ What brand voice guidelines apply?

WHILE WRITING, ENSURE:
□ Sounds human when read aloud
□ Uses "you" more than "we"
□ Clear and direct, not clever-cute
□ Matches the energy level needed
□ Appropriate for the audience
```
